Timeline

A lot of things happened so I could get where I am. Here is a timeline of some important events in my life:

1992

March 12: I was born in São José dos Campos, Brazil.

1994

With 2 years old, I had my first contact with a computer. It was a 386 processor with just some megabytes of HDD that ran Windows 3.1.

1996

Began to learn English language through a computer software. Also started to play some Strategy and RPG games (like Warcraft and Heroes of Might and Magic).

1997

Playing with little electronic parts like LEDs, engines, batteries and building my own toy cars.

In computer games, I become a fan of some FPS games (Doom, Duke Nukem 3D, Quake), and strategy games (Sim City 2000, Railroad Tycoon).

I was a great fan of Lego too. I used to build small cities with houses and monuments and imagine social, economic and military systems in it.

2001

Memorized the name and abilities of all the first 150 pokemons.

2002

In elementary school (in the 4th grade) I already showed mastery and passion about computers, and with the help of the teachers I suggested, prepared and gave classes of basic digital inclusion for my classmates, using the computer room of the school that was not being used.

My teacher also allowed me some weekly hours of the classroom, so I could teach basic chemistry to my classmates.

Began to read at least three books per week.

Started practicing Judo.

2004

My father (who is an engineer and helped me) allowed me to project entirely a house my parents built. I developed myself all the civil, electrical, hydraulic and structural plans along with the architecture.

I also had my first experiences playing with HTML and trying to build a static website about games.

Started practicing swimming.

Earned a medal in a regional chess championship.

2005

In 7th grade, I founded and organizing elections for the first School Students' Association. I also run for the elections and I was elected president.

I participated for the first time in the Brazilian Mathematical Olympics of Public Schools (OBMEP), getting a honor certificate and a year of CNPq Jr. Scientific Initiation scholarship and mentoring program in partnership with the Institute of Pure and Applied Mathematics (IMPA).

2006

I was reelected as president of the School Students Association.

My first contact with computer programming in VB.

Again I participated in the Brazilian Mathematical Olympics of Public Schools (OBMEP), getting another honor certificate, a medal, and another year of CNPq Jr. Scientific Initiation scholarship and mentoring program.

Took an entrance exam to Colegio Embraer Juarez Wanderley for high school, in which I was placed 4th position between more than 5,000 candidates. The school is ranked between the very best schools in Brazil.

2007

In my first year in Juarez Wanderley High School, I was responsible for the marketing and propaganda in the event "Feira de carreiras de engenharia" (Engineering careers fair), by managing the hotsite creation, flyers and brochures.

Inside high school, I began to participate in an Engineering program (with university-level studies and labs).

2008

Still in the high school, I managed a project that carried out the reforestation of an area in near the school: The soil was recuperated and more than 300 trees were planted.

I started my interests for photography and fashion.

2009

I developed and led a project to install a system in school locker rooms that reuses the water from showers into toilets, saving water.

Finishing High School, I took entrance exams to several universities, being admitted in practically all of them. So I choose University of Sao Paulo - USP.

2010

While 17 years old, I began to study Information Systems at University of Sao Paulo - the top ranked university in Latin America and between the best in the world.

In my first year at the university, I suggested and led the "1º Dia do Descarte Legal" (1st Day of Cool Recycling at USP), which was an event to raise awareness about the correct disposal of electronic waste and collect it from the university community. It was very successful and a program of e-waste disposal was created based on that.

In June I started working as a programmer at E-Val, in where I was for more than 3 years.

Within an university course project, I suggested changes that were implemented in the faculty website, turning the navigation accessible for visually impaired people.

2013

Was awarded a scholarship from CNPq (Brazilian federal government) covering all costs for exchange studies in University of Tampere, in Finland. So, I left my life in Brazil for a year.

I swam in frozen lakes and in the Arctic Ocean (during winter), watched metal bands and met Santa Claus, went to saunas and drove a huskies sledge.

While In Finland, I focused my studies in Information Visualization; Interactive Media Use and Users; E-Business Management; Requirements Engineering; and Finnish language courses.

2014

Still in Finland, I focused studying Principles of Usability, UX and UI; Design and Evaluation of Use Experiences for Web Services and Games; Interactive Media, Society and Culture; and Game Design.

I was accepted to work in two Demola projects. One about developing risks data visualization (Demola Spring), and another about an educational platform for the Finnish Ministry of Education (Demola Summer).

Travelled to Rome, Italy.

Worked remotely with mobile applications (iOS development of a game/platform platform that helps people learn about cultural situations).

Back to Brazil, I worked with CIMO (nowadays Finnish National Agency for Education) and University of Tampere as a PR assistant promoting Finnish education. I participated in a education fair (Euro-Pós) and gave a talk for more than 600 people.

Started working at PagSeguro UOL.

2016

Finished my Bachelor's degree in Information Systems and went to Toronto to present a peer-reviewed publication I wrote in 2015 at the 18th International Conference on Human-Computer Interaction (HCI International 2016).

I took participation in the Finland House in Rio, during the Olympic Games. There, I represented the School of Education of University of Tampere and worked as a PR assistant helping to promote transnational education services and talking about my previous experience in Finnish universities.

During the Salão do Estudante São Paulo (university students' fair), I helped FINNIPS to promote student recruitment and again answered student's questions about my experiences.

2017

I was awarded the University of Tampere Global Student Award for Academic Excellence Scholarship.

Travelled to Chapecó, in the South of Brazil.

Travelled to Penedo, the only Finnish colony in Brazil, and Itatiaia National Park.

Moved again to Tampere, Finland. Started my Master's Degree in Software Development in University of Tampere.

Started working with Demola Global team, taking care of development and infrastructure of web services as a System Specialist.

2019

Finished my thesis on Fair Team Recommendations for Multidisciplinary Projects with maximum grade (laudatur) and graduated as a Master of Science (Filosofian maisterin tutkinto).

Travelled to Nice, France, and Bergen, Norway.

Peer-reviewed publication at the IEEE/WIC/ACM International Conference on Web Intelligence.

Extending the work of my Master's thesis, started working for Tampere University as a part-time Project Researcher.
